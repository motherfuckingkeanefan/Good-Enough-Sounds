The Sounds of Landje van Bremmer

Location and Conditions

Landje van Bremmer is a small but ecologically valuable nature area near Wassenaar. Its biodiversity thrives due to a combination of natural soil conditions, minimal human interference, and careful land management. The open grasslands here are gradually transitioning into a more diverse habitat, rich in wildflowers and insect life, thanks to conservation efforts like reduced fertilization and strategic mowing. During my recording session, the tranquility was only interrupted by a chorus of enthusiastic geese and the occasional rustling of the reeds in the wind. It’s a space where nature is left to shape itself, creating a dynamic and ever-changing soundscape.

Recording Setup

This was my first-ever field recording! I used my go-to setup: a Zoom F3 recorder paired with Line Audio CM4 microphones in ORTF. Everything was mounted on a Manfrotto 5001B Nano Stand. A light breeze meant I had to use my Rode WS8 windscreens, which did a decent job—until a gust of wind decided to test my patience.

Technical Details

  • Coordinates: 52°10'24.8"N, 4°28'11.5"E
  • Date: March 6, 2025
  • Time: 15:45 – 16:45
  • Weather: Mild breeze, 14 km/h, 13°C, occasional sunshine
  • Microphones: Line Audio CM4 (ORTF setup)
  • Recorder: Zoom F3, 32-bit stereo

Observations

As expected, the geese stole the show. Their honking echoed through the reeds, creating an unplanned but oddly rhythmic soundscape. In between, I managed to capture softer sounds—the wind brushing through tall grasses and the occasional splash of a duck diving for breakfast. Nature is unpredictable, and that’s exactly why I love recording it.


Just a note: I only cut out a small portion from the 1-hour recording and am still learning the process of post-editing. There's a lot to learn, but it’s a rewarding experience, and I’m excited to continue exploring field recording.
