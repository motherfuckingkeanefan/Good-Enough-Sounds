Waves at Katwijk Beach

Location and Conditions

Recorded at Katwijk aan Zee, a coastal town in the Netherlands known for its open beaches and shifting tides. The conditions were mild, with an overcast sky and a light breeze coming in from the northwest.

Recording Setup

The microphones were placed in ORTF configuration on a K&M stereo bar, positioned close to the waterline but far enough to avoid splashes. No wind protection was used as conditions were stable.

Technical Details

  • Coordinates: 52.1975° N, 4.3986° E
  • Date: February 2025
  • Time: 17:30 CET
  • Weather: 8°C, overcast, wind NW at 8 km/h
  • Microphones: Line Audio CM4 (ORTF)
  • Recorder: Zoom F3 (32-bit float, 48 kHz)

Listen

Observations

This was recorded just before sunset, when the tide was rising. The waves were steady, breaking at a predictable rhythm. A few distant seagulls can be heard, but overall, this was a quiet moment on the beach.

The lack of strong wind helped keep the recording clean. The only interruptions were a few walkers passing by in the distance. No post-processing was applied.
