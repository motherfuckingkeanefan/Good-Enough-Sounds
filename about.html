About

My name is Pieter Houtekamer. I started this project as a way to stop overthinking and start recording. I have always been drawn to sound, but for a long time, I let self-doubt and the search for the perfect setup hold me back.

Starting Out

Like many beginners, I spent more time researching field recording than actually doing it. I convinced myself that I needed better gear, more knowledge, and the right conditions before I could start. I kept waiting for the perfect moment, but it never came.

Letting Go of Perfection

Good Enough Sounds is a way to move past that hesitation. Instead of chasing flawless recordings, I focus on documenting sound as it happens. Wind, background noise, unexpected interruptionsâ€”these are all part of the experience. The goal is not to edit reality but to capture it.

Where I Record

Most of my recordings are made around Katwijk aan Zee, a coastal town in the Netherlands. The sea, dunes, and changing weather provide an unpredictable soundscape that is never the same twice. I also record in nearby wetlands and quiet rural areas where birds and environmental sounds take over.

Approach

I use simple and reliable equipment to keep the process as straightforward as possible. Rather than focusing on perfect takes, I try to capture sound as naturally as possible with minimal processing.

Good Enough Sounds is a personal project, but I hope it resonates with others who are interested in sound, field recording, or the challenge of getting past perfectionism.
