About This Project

My name is Pieter. I started Good Enough Sounds as a way to stop overthinking and just start recording. Because if we wait for the perfect moment, we’ll be waiting until the universe runs out of cosmic gas.

The Problem with Perfection

Like many beginners, I spent more time reading gear reviews than actually pressing “Record.” I was convinced I needed a microphone blessed by angels, a noise-free environment, and the perfect alignment of the stars. But guess what? Waiting for all that basically means never recording at all.

Recording Without Fear

This project is about embracing imperfection—if there’s a gust of wind, a random bird cameo, or a passing ice-cream truck, it’s all part of the story. No over-polishing, no editing out reality, just documenting life as it unfolds.

Where I Record

Most of my recordings happen around Katwijk aan Zee, a coastal town in the Netherlands. Between the crashing waves, windy dunes, and the occasional grumpy seagull, there’s never a dull sonic moment. I also wander into wetlands and rural areas for that fresh “cow-mooing-in-the-distance” ambiance.

Approach

My philosophy? Keep it simple. I use reliable gear, minimal post-processing, and a hearty dose of “Let’s see what happens.” Because real life is messy, but that’s what makes it interesting.
