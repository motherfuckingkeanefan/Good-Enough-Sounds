My Recording Gear

Look, I love cool gadgets as much as the next audio nerd, but my setup is intentionally simple. I’d rather capture a spontaneous cow moo than fumble with 50 dials and miss the moment.

Microphones

  • Line Audio CM4 – Small-diaphragm condenser with a natural sound (and no special incantations required).

Recorders

  • Zoom F3 – 32-bit float, so I can record without worrying if a sudden donkey bray might clip the audio.

Support & Accessories

  • K&M 23550 Microphone Stereo Bar – Perfect for an ORTF setup, or for smacking away curious seagulls (just kidding).
  • K&M 85035 Microphone Holders – These keep my mics from flailing around in the wind.
  • Line Audio CMEA Elastic Adapter – Vibration isolation, so your footsteps don’t sound like thunder.
  • Manfrotto 5001B Nano Stand – Tiny in stature but mighty in stability.
  • Movo WST50 Windscreen – Because Dutch coastal winds do not play around.
  • Sony MDR-7506 Headphones – Crisp, clear audio, so you can catch every little detail, even when your fingers are frozen.

Recording Approach

I keep it lightweight. I’m not out to impress NASA with a 300-pound rig. The goal? Press record, see what happens, and maybe capture a few comedic moments along the way.
